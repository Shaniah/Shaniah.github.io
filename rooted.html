PROJECTS
ABOUT

ROLES: UI/UX DESIGNER, ILLUSTRATOR, BRAND IDENTITY

Streamlined chronic/mental illness health tracking application and notification system.

INTRODUCTION

Chronic and mental illnesses are very energy depleting, and can make it difficult and discouraging to track symptoms and find solutions to help manage them.

THE PROBLEM

Current apps on the market can take too much effort to track daily struggles for those with chronic and/or mental illness. It’s hard to keep track of medication, energy, food, water, symptoms (and more) in a streamlined way.

CONSTRAINTS
  • The design cannot expend as much energy as current apps on the market to use.
  • The initial onboarding must be simple and concise.
  • Research will be prone to bias due to scope of this project.
RESEARCH
DIARY STUDIES

The studies consisted of 3 participants, ages 22-50, men and women, across 3 different countries with varying diagnosed chronic and mental illness. The participants were asked to track specific habits at certain times, as well as track their food and water intake. These were their individual diagnosis.

COMPETITIVE ANALYSIS

Competitive analysis was conducted to find out what features exist on other apps/software on the market. Their weak point is that they take a lot of time and effort to track habits/symptoms.

INTERVIEWS

The interview consisted of 3 participants, ages 25-29, men and women, across 2 different countries. There were 7 questions asked with follow up questions to collect qualitative data.

KEY TAKEAWAYS
  • It is hard to continuously remember to open an app to track habits.
  • Certain wording in current apps on the market can be triggering. (ex: toxic positivity, diet culture).
  • Haptic feedback promotes positive feelings upon completion.
AUDIENCE

People who want to learn better ways to manage their mental and physical illness without expending too much time and energy.

WIREFRAMES

Initial layout and design of the app and notifications with a focus on research-based needs and desires in a reminder/tracking app (based on sketches, sitemap, and user flow).

BRANDING

The branding started a bit ambitious. Ultimately, the initial designs did not translate well and lacked readability. This design ideation was simplified by bringing an element of the leaves with analogous colors.

DESIGN

A calming color pallette with positive and soft colors to promote a motivational and soothing experience. Current iOS design rules analyzed for notification pop-ups.

FIRST ITERATION

Very quick set up process to get the app running and ready to send notifications to the user at their desired times. Sign up features for added tracking.

OPTIONS

Plenty of options based on user research and competitive analysis to cover any needs without the user needing to take time to create custom alerts.

TIME SELECTION

Streamlined time selection for people to not waste energy inputing exact times for each reminder, so the app can automatically spread them throughout the day.

SIMPLE NOTIFICATIONS

Throughout the day, notifications will appear on the screen of the person using the app. They can quickly document habits or health concerns with the tap of a button - or close them if they wish.

USER TESTING

Usability testing brought up a few issues with the app that needed addressing. Here are a few of the major changes that were made.

REMOVED SIGN-UP

Signing up adds an unnecessary extra step and should ideally be delayed in the process. Signing up was entirely replaced with a short description, and the option to learn more about the app or move on faster.

ONBOARDING

If someone needs more information about the app before inputing information, they can choose to read 2 pages of description to help clarify any confusion.

WORDING CHANGES

Some of the previous wording within the app was confusing. Changing sleep schedule to “desired” sleep schedule is less strict to the person’s livelihood.

ADDED ACCESSIBILITY

Added a small checkmark below selections made to be conscientious of people that may be colorblind or need additional visual cues.

SUMMARY
  • The final product is simple to use with a very quick set up process. The notification system allows people with chronic/mental illness to go about their day and not have to remember to open up the app to track habits, health concerns, or needs.
  • The wording is simple and doesn’t interject any ideals on the person using the app. The usage of icons helps remove the need for additional words.
  • The app has accesibility features to help with colorblindness.
  • Personalized features for everyone - without having to take the time to set up tedious custom alerts/notifications.
