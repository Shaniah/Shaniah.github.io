INTRODUCTION

Zeit is a fictional company looking to start up a business in booking time travel resort trips to the past. Time travel tourism is a fresh market with a lot of unknowns. Zeit has laid many ground rules for time travel tourism, which involves only being able to go to the past in specific locations/times where they have their resorts in place. Customers must travel to Zeit headquarters in Germany and will time travel from there.

RESEARCH

Innovation and creativity is a necessary skill when designing for the future. Products that currently do not exist are being imagined and created every day. That is what Zeit is all about. Researching for something that does not currently exist can prove difficult, however this was approached from multiple angles.

INTERVIEW

  • 6 participants
  • Ages 21-29
  • USA and Europe
  • Unemployed to $57k incomes
  • Limited to extensive experience in travel sites/apps
  • Well versed in tech

Research Goals

  • Determine what sites people currently use for traveling and why.
  • Understand the priorities of travel destination (cost, safety, weather, attractions).
  • Find out about any specific locations that are desireable.

Interview Findings

  • Cost-effectiveness and destination desireability are equally important, however even people that are cost-effective, would likely prefer to save up for an important trip.
  • Most people were more comfortable traveling with others and/or traveling to see others, than simply traveling alone for their own interest.
  • Cleanliness and location of the resort is favored rather than the quality/aesthetic.
  • Commonly used competitors: Trivago, Airbnb, National Express, Hopper and specific airline sites.
  • Enjoy having boarding passes all in one place digitally.

Key Takeaways

  • To increase the desireability of Zeit to people beyond the wealthy, it is important to have options that are more cost effective.
  • Offer group packs/activities on the site for people that want to travel, but donâ€™t want to feel alone.
  • Luxury and Utility options of lodging for those looking for a more comfortable trip vs adventurous.
  • Access room key and tickets on mobile to have everything in one place.

THE PROTOTYPE

6 participants tested the flow of the site and suggested minor changes to most of the flow. The booking sections required the most updates, since it also requires the most steps. Adding the ability to edit the order was the most crucial and important note from the usability testing.
