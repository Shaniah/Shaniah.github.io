PROJECTS
ABOUT

ROLES: UI/UX DESIGNER, ILLUSTRATOR

A new feature that incorporates group learning to promote better fluency and retention.

INTRODUCTION

Duolingo is the leading software to learn a language, that gamifies education by providing personalized exercises and challenges.*

*This is a concept project and is not affiliated with Duolingo. This is intended to simulate designing a new feature for a pre-existing product.
THE PROBLEM

Currently Duolingo is sitting at a B2 level of fluency (job-ready), and loses its more advanced learners.

CONSTRAINTS
  • Focus on free education - and must not create massive costs for Duolingo.
  • Provide a safe learning environment for potential minors using the app with a focus on features for adults to become more fluent.
  • Research will be prone to bias due to the scope of this project.
RESEARCH
COMPETITIVE ANALYSIS

Competitive analysis was conducted to find out what features exist on other apps/software on the market. Duolingo's weakest points are that it is particularly lacking in 1-on-1 and group features.

SURVEY

The survey consisted of 41 participants, ages 18-45, of all genders, across 9 different countries. There were 9 questions asked in this survey to obtain quantitative data. (These 3 questions represent only a portion of the survey).

INTERVIEWS

The interviews consisted of 6 participants, ages 25-29, men and women, across 3 different countries. There were 8 questions asked with follow up questions to collect qualitative data.

KEY TAKEAWAYS
  • Duolingo is missing group and 1-on-1 features that competitors have.
  • Participants prefered structured learning with an element of self-motivation.
  • Speaking proficiency was most desired among participants.
AUDIENCE

People that wish to enhance their language learning to connect better with others more personally and naturally.

WIREFRAMES

Initial layout and design of the group features with a focus on individual flow (based on sketches, sitemap, and user flow).

DESIGN

Old and new assets designed and created in Figma around Duolingo’s current branding and identity.

FIRST ITERATION

A focus on group sessions was designed based on interviews, where participants desired more group interactions to help provide better conversational fluency.

GROUP SESSIONS

Group calls that provide prompts to the users from their desired lesson to get further practice with pronunciation and conversation.

GROUP SAFETY

No 1-on-1 features added due to the potential of minors being in calls alone with an adult. (Even if the feature is age restricted).

USABILITY TESTING

Usability testing of the initial prototype brought about a few important details that were in line with the values and constraints of this new feature.

ADDITIONAL SAFETY

Safety features were implemented to protect people from uncomfortable situations and hold those responsible accountable for their actions.

FEATURES WITH FRIENDS

The ability to queue with friends for those that are too nervous to join calls alone.

STRUCTURED LEARNING

Further structure added to group calls in line with the research provided - where each person gets a chance to speak and mark when they are ready to move on. Blue outlines indicate when someone's mic is picking up sound.

SUMMARY
  • The product does not create massive cost to Duolingo due to no mentors being hired for lessons (like competitors). This keeps the product free for people who wish to use group lessons - while allowing a potential to add Duolingo Plus features.
  • Group sessions help keep minors safe should they use this feature (intended for adults).
  • Getting friends involved helps potentially bring more people to the app.
  • Conversational learning is important to increasing fluency and could make Duolingo even more reputable as a language learning software.
